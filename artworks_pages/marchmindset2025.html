Colors of the Waterfall: While in Rishikesh, I visited a waterfall. The colors in this painting are inspired by the shades I saw in the trees, flowing rivers, flowers, birds, sky, and butterflies around me. Me with Nature: In that place, I lost myself completely and felt deeply connected with nature. Rishikesh within me: As I gazed at the Ganga River, the surrounding mountains, trees, and the beauty of the sky in Rishikesh, I felt my heart surrender completely. My soul became one with nature, merging into the essence of that place. This painting is an expression of that love. The Love of a Swan: When I think of love, the image of a swan is what first comes to my mind. Blooming Emotions: Every day in Rishikesh brought a wave of different emotions. Some days, I felt energetic and full of excitement; other days, I experienced sadness, tears, longing, and solitude. In solitude, I realized - You are enough for Yourself: In solitude, I understood the beauty of self-reliance. No matter what, you will rise. Even in barren lands, flowers bloom. The Womb and The Seed: I once saw a pregnant woman, and something stirred deep within me. My emotions brought forth the image of a growing tree. Just like a tree begins from a seed, human life starts in the womb. Trees grow from seeds planted by humans, while human life is born from love and divine blessings. Expression of Love: This painting captures the emotions of a man and a woman in love. When in love, they lose themselves in a world of their own, forgetting everything around them. They float in the intoxication of love, sharing and embracing its warmth. Where is my Other Half?: Having faced heartbreak, I once told myself that I no longer needed love. But an experience in Rishikesh changed my perspective—it made me realize the true significance of love. Every human, whether man or woman, longs for a companion in their journey. In love, we all seek truth, sincerity, and the one who completes us—our missing half.
